Southbank Theater
a flexible music hall situated in the heart of London's cultural district


London, England


a foundation, terrace, and trove

Brutalism boasts its honesty and legibility in materiality and structure. The conceptual morphology of this design is a direct and honest translation of the function of each component of a theater.

As you walk across Queen's Walk, you catch a glimpse of a glimmering façade in the corridor between the Royal Festival Hall and the Hayward Gallery. You walk towards the building and slowly discover a set of terraces that lead to a glazed theater that seems to be majestically floating in the air. The terraces seemingly plug and attach onto adjacent buildings, slowly inhabiting the currently under-utilized void. Once you reach the top and enter the theater space, the view from the theater unfolds all of the terraced spaces, revealing the myraid of spaces you can venture, and a glorious view of the River Thames.

the foundation

The Foundation is a monolithic vertical structure that the back of house inhabits. The back of house is the foundation of a theater, supporting the artists and all that is presented to the patrons.

the terrace

The Terrace is a zig-zagging component of the structure that seeps into the house, inviting all who apporach it, inwards. The terrace connects to every existing level that connects to the new building, including two entrances at the front of the Royal Festival Hall, the Queen Elizabeth Hall, and the Hayward Gallery. Through this intervention, the Terrace connects both the morpohology and experience of the new theater to the rest of Southbank Centre.

the trove

The Trove is a glass box of a theater that seems to be afloat, which is supported, both metaphorically and structurally, by the Foundation. Inspired by the Appel Room at Lincoln Center, the Trove is glazed on one façade, allowing the theater to open up to the Terrace and the riverfront. Moreover, it should be considered a privilege to go to any theater, especially one at the Southbank Centre, thus, it is aligned at it's own axis, further highlighting the new theater's footprint, wedging itself into the void within Southbank Centre.
