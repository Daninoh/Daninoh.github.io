reading thoughts...


I always felt that there was something missing in my academic experiences learning about education and educational technologies. In his paper, Moulton describes the challenges that exist in the marriage between existing research and the practice of education. At first, I was reminded of Koedinger, Booth, & Klahr (2013) where they emphasize the complexities of learning science and develop a list of learning principles, but the more I read, the more I realized the focus was on finding the gaps in the bridge between practice and theory. It was refreshing to read something written about learning principles that isn’t obscure and verbose and really getting down to the essential (and extremely blunt) descriptions of the findings.


Beyond the summaries of the existing research, I found the Myths and Misconceptions section to be the most evocative. Namely that (1) “there is no evidence that teachers should design instruction based on students’ learning styles,” (2) “the concept of ‘far transfer’, or the doctrine of formal discipline—is highly suspect,” (3) “with some exceptions for elementary and special education, neuroscience adds very little value to our practical understanding of learning and teaching,” and (4) “as indirect measures of learning, student ratings of instruction are neither worthless nor particularly useful.”


Furthermore, I felt validated by the open questions and gaps Moulton points out, because many of these were the same questions I’ve been thinking about. These questions spanned from thinking about how we assess learners in different contexts to how we integrate evidence-based practices in classrooms and beyond to the trade-offs of having crammable lecture videos. All in all, I thought this paper provided a fantastic summary of known principles as well as a clear framework of potential research questions for educational researchers, educators, and learning designers.


my eportfolio


I spent a couple of hours setting up my ePortfolio for the Teaching and Learning Lab Practicum. This was a fun little design exercise, thinking about the most accessible format for the page. All in all, I’m happy with the current design, but open to future changes!




project choice


Currently, I am very interested in both the Anti-Racist Teaching Resources project and the Digital “Homepage” for New Online Program project. Both of these projects seem to complement my current skillset, hopefully allowing me to participate and contribute to the team to a professional degree. After speaking with Bill, I am a bit more drawn towards the latter project, due to its early project scope and the potential for a lot more design problem thinking.
